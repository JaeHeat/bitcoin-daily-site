Does the World Cup Mark the Bitcoin Bottom?

By Josh Molnar · July 2026 · 5 min read

Here is the chart taking over crypto this week. Bitcoin’s weekly candles, with a little gold World Cup trophy planted on the low of the 2022 crash, and a second trophy dropped right on this year’s dip near $58,000. The message is meant to be obvious. The World Cup shows up, Bitcoin bottoms, up only from here. The 2026 final ended on July 19, so the question is everywhere right now. Did the World Cup mark the Bitcoin bottom again? I pulled every World Cup in Bitcoin’s tradable history and lined each one up against the price. The record is not the one the meme is selling.

The World Cup Bitcoin bottom, tested on all four Cups

Bitcoin has traded through four World Cups. Here is what price did after each final whistle.

2014, Brazil. The final was July 13, Bitcoin at $630. If the World Cup marked the bottom, that was the floor. It was not. Price fell another 72 percent from there, all the way down to $176. The real low did not land until January 14, 2015, a full 185 days after the trophy was lifted. The World Cup was early by about six months.

2018, Russia. The final was July 15, Bitcoin at $6,348. Same movie. Price got cut in half again, down to $3,185. The low came on December 15, 2018, 153 days after the final. Five more months of bleeding after the tournament supposedly called it.

2022, Qatar. This is the meme’s whole case, and it comes with an asterisk. Qatar is the only World Cup ever played in November. FIFA moved it out of summer because of the desert heat. Bitcoin’s bear-market low formed that very same month, during the FTX collapse, and the tournament kicked off November 20 with Bitcoin at $16,247. The Cup and the bottom finally shook hands. It took the one tournament in history that got dragged into winter to do it.

2026. Kickoff on June 11 closed at $63,563. The final on July 19 closed at $64,682. The lowest the tournament ever printed was $58,524, on June 30. That dip is the second trophy on the viral chart. Whether it holds is the open question.

The real World Cup Bitcoin bottom scoreboard is 1 for 3

Three finished World Cups. Three chances to mark the bottom. It worked once. 2014 missed by six months. 2018 missed by five. Only 2022 hit, and it hit for a reason that has nothing to do with the meme.

Here is what the trophy chart leaves out. Every Bitcoin bear market has bottomed in the same narrow slice of the calendar. November 2011. January 2015. December 2018. November 2022. Four bottoms, every one of them between November and the middle of January. Deep winter. The 2022 World Cup did not find that window. It got moved into it.

Why every World Cup since 2014 kicked off in a crash

There is a real reason these two things keep landing near each other, and it is not football. It is two clocks running at almost the same speed.

Bitcoin moves on a roughly four-year rhythm, and its peaks keep landing late in the year. December 2013. December 2017. November 2021. Then October 6, 2025, at $124,824. The World Cup runs on its own four-year clock, and it lands the following summer. So the summer tournament always shows up months after a top, while Bitcoin is still falling. You can see it in how deep the hole already was on kickoff day.

Down 48 percent at kickoff in 2014. Down 66 percent in 2018. Down 76 percent in 2022. Down 49 percent in 2026. Every World Cup of Bitcoin’s life has kicked off inside a crash. Right year. Wrong month. I broke the full mechanism down in Bitcoin’s four-year cycle explained.

So where does that leave the 2026 Bitcoin bottom?

Honest answer. The July 19 final is almost certainly not the floor, for the same reason the last three summer World Cups were not. The history points somewhere else. The last three bear markets bottomed 406 days, then 364 days, then 366 days after their peaks. Call it 12 to 13 months, every time. This peak was October 6, 2025. Run that simple arithmetic and it points at late 2026, not a July whistle.

Fair warning on that. It is three data points, not a law of physics. I hold it loosely and so should you. It is enough to say the meme is pointing at the wrong month. It is not enough to circle a single date and bet the house. Our research on Bitcoin’s four-year clock and the live cycle tracker go deeper on the timing without pretending any of it is a guarantee. For the shorter version of why the summer green fools people, I wrote up why a green July 2026 is a trap.

Is the World Cup a real Bitcoin indicator? No, it is a calendar

What you are really staring at is two calendars that both repeat every four years. Stack two four-year cycles on top of each other and they will keep drifting near each other, over and over, forever. That is not Bitcoin waiting for the referee. That is arithmetic. Hand me any event that happens every four years, the Olympics, a leap year, a US election, and I can plant a trophy on a Bitcoin chart and sell you the exact same pattern. The World Cup version just has better marketing.

Four tournaments is not a track record. It is a coincidence in a nice costume. The bottom will arrive when the cycle says it arrives, not when somebody lifts a trophy. Screenshot the meme if you like it. Just do not size a single decision on it.

Common questions

Does the World Cup mark the Bitcoin bottom?

Not reliably. Across the four World Cups in Bitcoin's history, only 2022 landed near a bottom, and that was the one tournament ever moved to November, the season Bitcoin already bottoms in. In 2014 and 2018 the real low came five to six months after the final.

Did the 2022 World Cup call the Bitcoin bottom?

It lined up with it. Bitcoin's bear-market low formed in November 2022 during the FTX collapse, the same month Qatar kicked off. It is the meme's only hit, and it happened because that World Cup was the only one ever played in winter instead of summer.

When did Bitcoin bottom after past World Cups?

After the 2014 final Bitcoin kept falling and bottomed on January 14, 2015, about 185 days later. After the 2018 final it bottomed on December 15, 2018, about 153 days later. Both lows came the winter after a summer tournament.

When is the Bitcoin bottom expected in 2026?

The last three bear markets bottomed roughly 12 to 13 months after their peaks. This peak was October 6, 2025, which points toward late 2026 rather than the July World Cup final. That is a pattern drawn from three cycles, not a guarantee.

Is the World Cup a reliable Bitcoin indicator?

No. The World Cup and Bitcoin's four-year cycle both repeat every four years, so they drift near each other by coincidence. Four tournaments is far too small a sample to be a signal, and the one hit only worked because that Cup was played in Bitcoin's usual bottom season.
