How to Make Money in a Bear Market (Without Calling the Bottom)

By Josh Molnar · June 2026 · 6 min read

Every bear market, my DMs fill with the same question: when is the bottom? Wrong question. You do not need to call the bottom to make money in a bear market. I have not called one all year, and I am up roughly 34% while Bitcoin is down about 50%. Here is how that math is even possible.

Calling the bottom is a coin flip. Stop building your plan on it.

Picking the exact low is guessing dressed up as analysis. Even when you are right, you are usually early, and early in a bear is the same as wrong for months. So I stopped trying. The goal is not to be the genius who nailed the bottom tick. The goal is to still have an account, and a bigger one, when the cycle actually turns.

Run two books, not one

Most people treat “trader” and “holder” like a personality test you have to pick. They are not. I run two separate books, and they do not contradict each other.

  • The macro book is patient money. It sits mostly in cash through the bear and buys on a plan, not a feeling. Cash is a position. It is the position that lets you buy when everyone else is forced to sell.
  • The trading book is active money. Short-term setups, long and short, sized so no single trade can hurt me. This book does not care which direction the market goes. It cares that the market moves.

The bear funds the trading book and builds the macro book’s patience at the same time. They stack. They do not fight.

How the trading book makes money while the chart bleeds

A falling market is not an absence of opportunity. It is a market that moves, and movement is the raw material a trader works with. Two things make this book work:

  • Both directions. If you can only profit when price goes up, half the year is closed for business. Learn to short, or at least to stand aside, and the chop becomes income instead of pain.
  • Fixed risk. I risk the same small percentage on every trade, win or lose. That one rule is why a string of losers is an annoyance instead of a funeral. Most people do not blow up because they are wrong. They blow up because one trade was too big.

How the macro book makes money when the cycle turns

The macro book does almost nothing for most of the bear, and that is the hard part. Doing nothing feels like missing out. It is not. Bottoms get built in stages: the speculation dies first, the forced sellers leave next, and the floor forms while everyone is too exhausted to care. You do not buy the headline that feels like a bottom. You buy on a plan you wrote in advance, when the math lines up, and you do it in tranches so you are never betting the whole account on a single day.

The one rule that ties it together

Whether you are trading the chop or accumulating for the cycle, the entire game is risk. Position sizing is the strategy. Your stop is not a safety net you hope you never touch. It is the line that defines the trade before you ever click buy. Get sizing right and you can be wrong over and over and still be standing. Get it wrong and being right once will not save you.

The takeaway

  • You do not need to call the bottom. You need a plan that pays whether or not you do.
  • Two books: trade the chop with fixed risk, accumulate the cycle with patient cash. They stack, they do not contradict.
  • Risk per trade is the whole edge. Survive the bear with your account intact and the next cycle does the heavy lifting.
