The band is only three past cycles, so treat the edges as a rough envelope, not a hard floor or ceiling. It shows how this bear compares in shape, not where the exact bottom will land.

Every Bitcoin bear market feels unprecedented while you are living through it. Stacking them on top of each other tells a calmer story. This chart takes the three completed post-top bears (2013, 2017 and 2021), lines them up at their peak, and measures each one as a fraction of its own top. Collapse those three lines into the space between the highest and the lowest at each point and you get a shaded band: the historical range of where Bitcoin has traded at every stage of a bear.

The orange line is the current cycle, measured the same way and dropped inside the band. When it rides the top edge, this bear is shallower than history. When it sinks toward the bottom edge, it is tracking the worst of the past. The dashed line through the middle is the average bear, a rough base case for how a normal cycle has behaved.

So far this cycle has held above the historical range, part of the same maturation you see across these charts: the swings are getting smaller as Bitcoin grows up. The honest caveat is that three cycles is a small sample, so read the band as a shape guide, not a guarantee. It tells you whether this bear is behaving normally, not the day it ends.
