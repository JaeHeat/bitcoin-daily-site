The Radar weighs the market forces that decide whether money favors Bitcoin or altcoins into one live score. We keep the exact recipe proprietary, but the result is public. In a six-year forward test it read what altcoins did next better than the standard altcoin season index, which lands on a coin flip. It is a higher-conviction read of the regime you are in right now, not a predictor of the next altcoin season, if we even get another one.

Altcoin season is really one question. Is money staying with Bitcoin, or is it spilling out into the rest of the market. The Altseason Radar reads the forces that answer that question and folds them into a single score from 0 to 100. Low means Bitcoin is in control. High means capital is rotating into altcoins. The exact recipe is ours, but the score updates live and anyone can watch it move.

We built it because the tool everyone already uses, the Altcoin Season Index, is a rear-view mirror. It scores how alts did over the last 90 days, so by the time it flashes green the move is mostly over. We put both through the same forward test across six years. When the index said altcoin season, altcoins beat Bitcoin over the next month only about half the time, a coin flip. The Radar did meaningfully better. That does not make it a crystal ball, and a true altcoin season may not return the way it used to, but as a live read of where things stand it beats the standard the whole market leans on.

Read it as a fast, honest gauge of whether the wind is at altcoins' back or in their face right now. It tracks the regime you are already in rather than forecasting the next one, and it is one input, not the whole picture. Use it alongside the other signals on this site.
