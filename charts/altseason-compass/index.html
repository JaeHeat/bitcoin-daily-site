Built from the two signals that explain most of what alts do: Bitcoin dominance direction and BTC price direction. Stablecoin dominance is left out on purpose, our own testing showed it adds almost nothing once you already know where price is. The compass reads the regime you are in, it does not predict the next one.

Altcoin performance is decided mostly by two things at once: whether money is rotating out of Bitcoin into the rest of the market, and whether the market is rising at all. The Altseason Compass folds those two into a single score from 0 to 100. Bitcoin dominance falling is the rotation signal. BTC price rising is the risk-on signal. When both line up, the score climbs toward 100, and that is when altcoins have historically had their strongest runs.

The four states map onto a simple cheat sheet. Dominance falling while price rises is alt season, the green zone up top. Dominance rising while price falls is risk-off, the red zone at the bottom, where alts fall harder than Bitcoin itself. The two mixed states, bitcoin season (both rising) and rotation (both falling), sit in the middle. The colored line and the live reading tell you which one is in force right now.

It is a dashboard, not a crystal ball. Because dominance and price are the same ingredients that move altcoins, the score tracks the regime you are already in rather than forecasting the next one. We tested it honestly: as a timing tool it mostly helps you avoid altcoin drawdowns rather than beat simply holding Bitcoin. Read it as a fast, honest gauge of whether the wind is at altcoins' back or in their face, and confirm it against the other signals here.
